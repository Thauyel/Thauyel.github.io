PROJECT_VIEW_TREMORA

Tremora

"A Safe Haven"

Location: Şişli, Istanbul

Type: Emergency Response / Modular

Year: 2024

Role: Concept & System Design

View 360° Panorama

A safe haven in times of need, a community hub every day. This project aims deliver a relief home structure to support a modular assembly system. The building's core components are designed for rapid assembly and disassembly, allowing for an efficient transformation from a community center to an emergency response hub.

The modular units can be used simultaneously from world gatherings to crisis response, disaster recovery, and relief efforts as community-centric response scenarios.

PROJECT_VIEW_TRAVION

Travion

"Trade, Travel & Transform"

Location: Kemeraltı, Konak, Izmir

Type: Public Hub / Historical Bazaar

Year: 2023

Role: Architectural Design & Contextual Analysis

A hub solution for Izmir/Kemeraltı residents that serves as both a resting place and a passageway, seamlessly integrating modern amenities with the rich history of the area. Travion includes a public square, a bazaar area, and an auction house, all infused with historical elements that pay homage to Kemeraltı's vibrant trading tradition.

By modernizing these existing trade functions, the project creates a dynamic and versatile space that caters to contemporary needs while preserving cultural heritage. The hub serves as a gateway to the historic market, providing both entrance and exit points, making it a central and functional part of the community where people can pause, interact, and engage with their surroundings.

PROJECT_VIEW_HERMES

Hermes

"Move, Play, Thrive!"

Location: Gebze, Kocaeli / Turkey (Former Quarry)

Type: Recreational Hub / Landscape Integration

Year: 2025

Role: Architectural Design & Landscape Integration

Project Video

Located within the confines of a former quarry, Hermes reinterprets an artificial landscape through deliberate architectural intervention. The design accepts the site's post-industrial condition and engages in active reshaping, introducing a dual-axis organizational strategy that guides visitors through open recreational programs while accommodating controlled-access functions.

The modular structure of steel and sandwich panels extends downward, adapting to the topographic slope and transforming the quarry into a dynamic recreational hub. Rather than merely occupying the space, the building dialogues with the evolving landscape, serving as a constructed memory that mediates between the visible and hidden, the active and contemplative.
